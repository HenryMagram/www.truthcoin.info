What is Truthcoin?

Bitcoin "Smart Contracts" (as Smart as Whatever's Reading Them)

Truthcoin is a Peer to Peer Oracle Protocol which absorbs accurate data into a blockchain so that Bitcoin-users can speculate in Prediction Markets. These markets have many applications (which include superior alternatives to Lighthouse, Counterparty, Medici, Bitshares, and Ethereum) and have the potential to revolutionize the emergance and diffusion of knowledge in society.

If you've never even heard of Bitcoin before, try What is Truthcoin? (10 fun slides) to learn about what you've been missing this whole time.

Then hit the papers and presentations, starting with: Why do this?. Feel free to browse the dev section or the forums.

Bitcoin Maximal

Planned to be a sidechain of Bitcoin. This means that Bitcoin (and only Bitcoin) is used to create and trade in Markets. A separate coin-type does some pretty fancy things to ensure that a group of self-selected, anonymous, greedy users will always resolve the contract-outcomes honestly.

The sidechains graphic from http://blockstream.com.

The Goal: Scientific Revolution Part II

Speaking technically, this project aims to get external data into a blockchain reliably, and use that data efficiently and conservatively.

However, unlike other blockchain-projects, the ultimate purpose is NOT to establish a new payment system (nor to delude the innocent with get-rich-quick nonsense), but instead to...

  • ...revolutionize the way information is created and spread, giving the internet a "frontal lobe"...
  • ...overhaul democratic governance (deemphasizing showmanship/PR, highlighting the irrelevance of race/gender/who-candidate-has-slept-with)...
  • ...boost economic growth...
  • ...provide a novel way to efficiently fund public goods, without taxation or coercion...
  • ...cut out all Wall St. / banking middlemen and replace them with tech...
  • ...spearhead the campaign to make Bitcoin the world's internet currency...
  • ...and more!

Bitcoin Projects I Like

BitPay and Purse.Io meet even my high standards, as they reveal inefficencies in traditional payment systems and send those savings to you, the user (as does Gyft). This is the so-called "fundamental value" of Bitcoin, end of story.

I imagined Bitcoin's 'killer app' to be something that would turn all phones and computers into ISPs as well as internet users, and make the internet fully private, cheap, and efficient (with too many benefits to list). BitMesh is working on something which might become this (as is OnionTip to some extent).

For Bitcoin wallets, I recommend Electrum (starting it up with the internet off [or on a really old laptop], writing down the 12 word seed, encrypting the seed with a huge passphrase [which you can immediately forget], reconnecting the internet, emailing yourself your new BTC addresses [which you watch from Blockchain.info], and finally erasing the installation completely). I also recommend the concept of Hardware Wallets, but I lack the CS expertise required to make a specific recommendation (although the Trezor looks good).
